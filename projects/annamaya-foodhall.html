Our Philosophy

Food as Magic,
Sourced with Soul

AnnaMaya is named after two goddesses: Annapurna — the goddess of nourishment — and Maya — the goddess of magic. Together they give birth to a foodhall that is more than dining; it is a conscious celebration of India's artisans, farmers, and food-makers.

"Our chefs spent a day at Tijara Farm to understand the finer nuances of seasonal produce — going beyond farm-to-table to celebrate the farmers and their food."
— Chef Vikram Ganpule, Executive Chef

Old Delhi's soul — hand-painted glass windows, sacks of spices, weathered wooden doors — meets a European food-hall floor of brown-and-white geometric tiles. Every square metre tells a story of where India's ingredients come from.

The AnnaMaya
Artisan Brunch

Not your typical Sunday brunch. AnnaMaya's Artisan Brunch is a curated slow-food experience — live stations, seasonal spreads from Tijara Farm, artisan breads baked that morning, and cocktails built around the week's freshest produce.

Voted one of Delhi's most celebrated Sunday rituals, it draws food lovers, families, and farm-to-fork advocates every week without fail.
