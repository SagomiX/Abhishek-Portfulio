The Story of Bukhara

A Legend Born
from a Campfire in 1978

The origins of Bukhara trace back to the rugged terrains and spirited camaraderie of the North-West Frontier Province — the borderlands of Afghanistan and undivided India. When ITC Maurya opened in 1978, Chairman A.N. Haksar carved a simple kabab restaurant from the hotel's kitchen space, inspired by the spirit of communal eating around a frontier campfire. He nearly named it Samarkand.

From the day it opened, Bukhara was Delhi's most sought-after table. It took no reservations after 8pm. There was always a queue outside. Half a century later, the queues are just as long — and the menu has barely changed a word since 1978.

"The grandmother revealed that her mother used to dine here in the early 1980s. She has done so all her life with her children, and now she's here with her grandson — and the food has evoked the exact same sentiments, always."

— Slurrp Magazine, 2024

The Dining Experience

No Cutlery.
Wear the Apron.
Eat with Your Hands.

In a novel twist that has captured the hearts of visitors from every nation, guests at Bukhara are presented with bib-like aprons upon seating — an invitation to embrace the tactile joy of eating with their hands, exactly as people ate around frontier campfires centuries ago.

🔥

Five Custom Clay Tandoors

Live tandoors visible from every table — watch chefs skewer and char over direct flame with precision honed over decades

Menu Unchanged Since 1978
