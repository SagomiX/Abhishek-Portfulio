Since 1948

A Legacy Born
From Partition

In 1947, Kasturi Lal Wadhwa and his wife Raj Rani fled Rawalpindi with nothing but a family of twelve and a recipe in their hearts. On the streets near India Gate, they began selling Pindi Chane from a cart — a signature recipe from Rawalpindi that Delhi had never tasted before.

People queued for the dish. In 1948, when the municipal corporation cleared the carts, Wadhwa was allotted a small shop on Pandara Road — making Pindi the very first restaurant in what would become Delhi's most famous food market.

"His butter chicken was covered by The New York Times in the 1980s. His Pindi Chane became the soul of Pandara Road."

— As reported by Outlook Traveller

Today, three generations later, Pindi continues to serve the same recipes — measured not by equipment but by hand, mood and memory — exactly as Wadhwa ji would have done.

Pindi Chana — The Dish That Started It All

The original recipe from Rawalpindi — chickpeas slow-cooked in a tangy, smoky masala with dried pomegranate and whole spices. Kasturi Lal Wadhwa started selling this from a cart at India Gate in 1947. It is still made the same way today. Must try with a hot kulcha.
